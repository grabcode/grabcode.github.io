## The Vision

Backseat was the **first ride-sharing solution in Sydney, Australia**. Born during the early days of the sharing economy, it connected drivers and passengers for affordable, community-driven urban transport — before the ride-sharing market exploded.

## My Role & Impact

As co-founder and CTO, I was involved from day one — focusing on the **product from user experience through to implementation and delivery** on both iOS and Android. I owned the full technical stack, made architecture decisions, and shipped cross-platform mobile apps while managing the designer and support team.

This was hands-on startup work: designing the backend, building the mobile experience, and iterating rapidly based on real user feedback from Sydney's commuting community.

## Tech Stack

Built on a full-stack JavaScript architecture:
- **Frontend**: Angular.js for responsive web interfaces
- **Mobile**: PhoneGap for iOS and Android cross-platform deployment
- **Backend**: SailsJS with Node.js for real-time ride coordination
- **Database**: MongoDB for flexible, scalable data storage
- **Build**: Grunt for asset pipeline and automation

## Key Features

- **Real-time matching**: WebSocket-powered ride requests and driver acceptance
- **Location tracking**: GPS integration for live driver and passenger positioning
- **Cross-platform apps**: iOS and Android delivery through PhoneGap
- **Rating system**: Community trust through peer reviews
- **Driver & passenger profiles**: Identity and reputation management

## The Journey

Backseat was a pivotal moment — moving from developer to **product builder and company co-founder**. It was an intense, fast-paced 9 months that taught me about shipping products under constraints, managing technical debt in a startup, and the importance of user empathy in product design. The experience directly shaped my path into subsequent ventures like Liftango.
